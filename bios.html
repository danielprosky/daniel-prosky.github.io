IASON TOGIAS | THE POET

Iason Togias is honored to be making his DC debut in a play so close to his heart. Select credits: King Richard in Richard II (Columbia University, MFA Directing); Banquo in Macbeth (Brick Theater); Troilus in Troilus and Cressida, Thomas More in Sir Thomas More (The Royal Academy of Dramatic Art); Vanya in Vanya and Sonia and Masha and Spike, Hamlet in Hamlet: The First Quarto, Leontes in The Winter’s Tale, Joe Keller in All My Sons, Antony in Antony and Cleopatra, Alan Turing in Breaking the Code, and the Duke in Measure for Measure (Yale University). Training: Yale, RADA. Iason can be reached at iasontogias@gmail.com.

CONOR BAGLEY | DIRECTOR & PRODUCER

Conor Bagley is delighted to bring this beautiful play to his hometown. Directing: Brian Friel's Dancing at Lughnasa, Conor McPherson's Shining City (Yale). Assistant Directing: The Home Place (Irish Repertory Theatre, New York premiere), Donegal (Abbey Theatre, Dublin, world premiere), Other Desert Cities (Theatre Workshop of Nantucket). Producing: Broadway: Once on this Island (Tony nomination). Recipient of the V. Browne Irish Prize for excellence in the performing arts at Yale and a 2018 Tony Award nomination for Best Revival of a Musical. Conor can be reached at conor.bagley@gmail.com.

DANIEL PROSKY | PRODUCTION DESIGNER

Daniel Prosky is a New York-based set designer. He has designed shows in unlikely places, like Ragtime on Ellis Island, Story Bank in the former Williamsburg Savings Bank, and Little Murders in a vacant burger place. Work in traditional spaces includes: Set design for The Other Kids Ran Away and Leni & Joseph (New Ohio), The Gleaming (Dixon Place), Flea Fridays (The Flea Theater) and lighting design for Wheelchair (Dixon Place), Antigone (Schapiro Studio), Houseworld Presents: The Sensitive Ear and 1969: The Second Man (ANT Fest, Ars Nova). Work in Cambridge, MA includes scenic design for The Pulitzer Centennial Celebration (Sanders Theater) and Bride*Widow*Hag (American Repertory Theater Institute). He received his B.A. from Harvard University. danielprosky.com
